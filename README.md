<h1><strong>LZTR1 Signaling Pathway Map</strong></h1>

##**Interactive Visualization for the TuRex Project**
This interactive pathway map provides a detailed visualization of the LZTR1 signaling network, focusing on its role in RASopathies and oncogenesis. This tool allows researchers to explore gene functions and interactions through direct integration with external biological databases.

###**Project Context**
This interactive visualization was developed as part of the TuRex Project at the Institute of Medical Genetics and Applied Genomics, University Hospital Tübingen (Universitätsklinikum Tübingen). The project is dedicated to mapping complex genetic interactions to unravel the underlying mechanisms of rare diseases and tumor predisposition.

####The Challenge: Silent Drivers in Oncology
A critical paradox in neuro-oncology is that approximately 90% of brain tumors are driven by overactive Receptor Tyrosine Kinase (RTK) signaling pathways, yet the RTK genes themselves frequently lack somatic mutations (1). Research has identified that STAT3-enhancing germline receptor variants (STAT3-eGRVs) are present across many RTKs, contributing significantly to this overactivity (2, 3, 4).

####The Role of LZTR1
Central to this regulatory network is LZTR1 (Leucine Zipper-like Transcription Regulator 1). LZTR1 serves as a master regulator by facilitating the polyubiquitination and subsequent degradation of RAS-GTPases. This mechanism allows LZTR1 to exert profound control over RTK signaling flux, even in instances where the RTK genes remain mutation-free (5).

####Clinical Implications
Mapping the LZTR1-dependent signaling pathways is therefore a therapeutic necessity. By defining these interactions, the TuRex Project aims to provide the framework required to evaluate the clinical impact of STAT3-eGRVs and identify new vulnerabilities in tumor progression..

###**Features**
Gene-Protein Integration: All gene symbols (HGNC approved) are hyperlinked directly to their respective UniProt entries, specifically targeting the Subcellular Location section for rapid functional lookup.

Interaction Mapping: Pathway connection lines are hyperlinked to specific PubMed IDs, providing immediate access to the peer-reviewed literature supporting the shown interaction.

High Fidelity: The layout preserves the original coordinate system and aesthetic of the high-resolution pathway diagram while adding a functional web layer.

###**Technical Details**
Format: Interactive HTML/CSS (Layered Absolute Positioning).

Data Sources: UniProt (Protein data), PubMed (Interaction evidence), HGNC (Nomenclature).

####How to use
Genes: Click on any gene name (e.g., LZTR1, KRAS, SHOC2) to view detailed protein information on UniProt.

Interactions: Click on the connection lines between proteins to open the primary literature supporting that specific interaction on PubMed.
